Things to Do

Duquesne or Monongehela Incline and Mt. Washington

Pittsburgh's two inclines leading up to Mount Washington are certainly a tourist favorite! The views from the top are amazing, especially at night, and the antique cable cars are also very fun to ride! At the bottom of the Monongehela Incline is Station Square, where you'll find a T station on the line that goes right past the Wyndham. At the top is wider array of restaurants and overlooks, although some of the more famous Mt. Washington restaurants are near the Duquesne Incline. Both inclines are also a half hour walk from the hotel.

Point State Park

Point State Park is a great place to visit if you have an hour or two to spare, as it's right across the street from the Wyndham! Be sure to check out the fountain at the Point to see where the Allegheny and the Monongehela Rivers join to form the Ohio. The diamond-shaped patterns in the grass are outlines of the two forts that once stood at the Point: Fort Pitt and Fort Duquesne.

The Strip District

While visiting the Strip District, check out some of the most unique food shops in Pittsburgh like Pennsylvania Macaroni Company, Grandpa Joe's Candy Shop, Wholey Fish Market, Lotus Food Market, Penn Avenue Fish Company, Parma Sausage Company, Allegheny Coffee and Tea Exchange, and more! The Strip District is also home to a ton of great restaurants like one of Danielle's favorites, Kaya, or the Pittsburgh brunch staple, Pamela's Diner. Although, if you'd like to visit Pamela's, we recommend crossing the river to their original restaurant in Millvale where the wait will be much shorter.

Mattress Factory Museum of Contemporary Art

The Mattress Factory is one of the most unique museums in Pittsburgh. Different than a traditional art museum, it has installation art exhibits which often encompass the entire room and are created specifically for that room. Also check out nearby Randyland where an artist has turned his home and yard into a colorful and eclectic art exhibit.

Craft Beer, Cider, and Whiskey

Pittsburgh is home to many great breweries, such as Dancing Gnome (great IPAs), Strange Roots (great sours beers), or Hitchhiker (a good variety). Click Here for a map of Pittsburgh breweries. Also check out Wigle Whiskey and Threadbare Cider if you get tired of beer!

Phipps Consevatory & Botanical Gardens

Phipps Conservatory is right at the edge of Schenley Park in the Oakland Neighborhood. Since it was so close to Pitt, and offered free admission to students, John would occasionally come here to study - especially in the middle of winter!
